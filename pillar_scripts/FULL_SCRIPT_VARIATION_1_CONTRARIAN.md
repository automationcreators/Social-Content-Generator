## WHY PROJECT MANAGEMENT EXISTED (1:00 - 3:00)

### The Original Constraints

Project management was invented to solve a specific problem:

**Limited resources + Complex work = Need for coordination**

**[VISUAL: Factory assembly line from 1950s]**

In the 1950s, you had two approaches:

**1. Assembly Line (Henry Ford)**
- Sequential work
- Each person does one task
- Batch processing
- Optimized for speed
- High upfront cost, low per-unit cost

**2. Toyota Way (Pull System)**
- Just-in-time production
- Parallel work streams
- Optimized for flexibility
- Lower upfront cost, higher per-unit cost

**[VISUAL: Assembly line vs. Toyota pull system diagrams]**

And you had to choose. You couldn't do both.

**Why?**

Because **human labor is expensive and time-bound.**

**[VISUAL: Cost breakdown showing human hourly rates]**

If you wanted to parallelize work (multiple people working simultaneously), you paid for multiple people's time.

If you wanted to batch work (one person doing many tasks), you saved money but lost speed.

**Time OR Money. Pick one.**

### Enter: Project Management

The entire discipline of PM exists to manage this trade-off:

**[VISUAL: PM triangle - Scope, Time, Cost]**

- **Critical path analysis:** Which tasks can run in parallel? (Minimizes time, increases cost)
- **Resource allocation:** Who should do what when? (Balances cost and time)
- **Gantt charts:** Visualizing the optimal sequence to minimize bottlenecks
- **Sprint planning:** Batching work into time-boxed chunks

All of it designed around one core assumption:

**Labor is constrained, expensive, and sequential.**

**[VISUAL: "LABOR = CONSTRAINT" in large text]**
